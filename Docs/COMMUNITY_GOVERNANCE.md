# Community Governance — Grasp

This charter turns ethical commitments into **repeatable processes**. It complements [`MARKET_WHOLESOMENESS_PLAN.md`](MARKET_WHOLESOMENESS_PLAN.md) and [`FOUNDERS_PLEDGE.md`](FOUNDERS_PLEDGE.md).

---

## Transparent roadmap (GitHub)

- **Feature requests** use issues with labels: `considering`, `planned`, `rejected`.
- **Rejections** include a short reason (example: *Rejected: requires cloud connectivity or breaks air-gap story*).
- **Quarterly** roadmap notes go in GitHub **Discussions** (or a pinned issue), summarizing what shipped and what did not.

---

## User council

- **Annual** survey of up to **100** power users (volunteers or invite-only).
- **Published** aggregate results only (no individual identification).
- One channel (email or form) listed in the README for structured feedback.

---

## Security audits (air-gap claim)

- **Goal:** Independent verification that core flows do not exfiltrate user content to Grasp-operated infrastructure.
- **Cadence:** At least **annual** when resources allow.
- **Deliverable:** A short published summary; technical detail may be redacted where it would harm users (e.g. exact exploit steps).

---

## Succession and source escrow

- **Trigger:** If there is **no maintained release** and **no responsive security communication** for **12 consecutive months**, activate the succession path.
- **Intent:** Release source to **verified purchasers** or place it with a neutral steward (e.g. Software Freedom Conservancy–style arrangement). Exact legal mechanism should be finalized with counsel before marketing the promise broadly.
- **Today:** This document states **intent**; binding escrow terms require a separate legal agreement.

---

## Anti-patterns (explicit)

- No cloud sync “for convenience.”
- No optional account for backup.
- No “AI improvement program” that uploads samples.
- No race-to-the-bottom pricing that makes maintenance impossible without compromising quality.
